Why three embeddings?
Token = what the word is. Position = where it sits. Segment = which sentence (A or B). All three are summed element-wise before the first encoder block.
Position: learned vs sinusoidal
Original Transformer used fixed sinusoidal patterns. BERT learns position embeddings from data — more flexible, adapts to the specific language seen during pre-training.

The 15% split
Why random replacement?
If only [MASK] was used, BERT would over-specialize. Random replacement (10%) forces BERT to check every token. Keeping original (10%) forces good representations everywhere — not just at mask positions.
Contextual embeddings in action
The same word gets a different 768-dim vector based on surrounding context. This is what BERT's "understanding" looks like.
Choose a polysemous word
Word2Vec assigns one fixed vector per word. BERT assigns a dynamic vector influenced by every other token in the sentence — this contextual shift is why BERT revolutionised NLP.
